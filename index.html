About

I am a web developer, working primarily in the Javascript stack. I've written Node.js and Python/Django APIs, and React, jQuery, and pure Javascript front-ends.

After getting a PhD in philosophy, I picked up Python as—I thought—a way of saving myself some time. What started as a project to simply reorganize a chaotic file-naming system turned into passion. As a philosopher, I'd honed my abilities to conceptualize a problem, and break it into ever simpler puzzles until landing on something tractable. As a developer employing those same skills, I discovered the joy of building something and actually seeing the results play out on the screen. So, I learned and I built, and just kept learning and building.

Since that time trying to save myself an hour's work, I've poured countless more hours into discovering new ways to create tools that other people might actually find useful—might even enjoy using. I haven't stopped relishing the challenges, and pushing myself to learn and grow as a developer.

When I'm not puzzling through code, I am happily occupied playing tactical games, bicycling, and talking philosophy.

If you want to get in touch, you can reach me at mattgif@gmail.com.
